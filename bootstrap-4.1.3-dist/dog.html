Dogs Up For Adoption

If interested in adopting any of the dogs or puppies you see today please view our pet adoption policy.


Sadie

Sadie is a three year old lab mix. Her gentle personality would make a great fit for a family with kids. She loves walks in the park and gets along great with other dogs and cats.

Lucy

Lucy's happy-go-lucky personality shines through from the first time you meet her. She's five years young and is the first one to the door for a walk. As you can see she loves snow and all things winter.

Madison

Madison is a wonderful lap dog. She enjoys playing with the other dogs here and is often found curled up with one of the cats here at the shelter. She's just a little over one year old and is already learning how to perform tricks.

Rudy

Rudy is a two year old poodle mix that has a zest for life. He creates excitement wherever he goes. In fact it has been observed that anywhere Rudy goes, there is a party.

Jupiter

Jupiter was found as a stray that showed up outside a gas station. He had an open wound and was taken to the vet promptly after arriving. After two months, he is all healed up and excited to find a forever home.

Darcy

Darcy is one of our special dogs here at the shelter. She has a lot of love to give and requires heart medication due to a heart murmur that was detected after she arrived. She's seven years old and will let you know when it is treat time.

Max

Max is a four month old puppy that has tons of energy. He loves to go to the park and fetch any kind of stick he can find—even when one wasn't thrown. He likes to be around other animals and would make a great family pet.

Mosley

Mosley is a six month old puppy and is growing fast. He enjoys playing with his toys and is often found lining them all up in his dog bed. He likes to be held and will stand on his back legs to let you know when.

Nellie

Nellie is almost three months old and is the newest edition here at Fur Friends. She loves getting baths and will run and jump right in. Her favorite sport is to to dart around and underneath the other dogs here at the shelter. We call it the Nellie olympics!
