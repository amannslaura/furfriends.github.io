The Truth About Black Animals: Why You Should Care

December 4, 2022

If I asked what color you associated recognition, power, and elegance with—how would you answer? If you said black, you’d be correct! Unfortunately, when associating black with animals—they are thought of as common and boring.

But are they? Why do we need to change the stigma of black pets?

Opening My Eyes to the Fate of Many Black Animals

The first time I had ever been informed about the struggles black animals face, was on a t-shirt waiting in line at the happiest place on earth. After asking the wearer a few questions, it opened my eyes to the plight black animals have endured opposite of their more “colorful” counterparts (and often litter mates).The awful fate of being picked last for adoption or worse yet to be added to the large number of other black animals that are euthanized each year.

We all know there are many animals each year shelters are trying to place in homes. Many work to place all adoptable animals, no matter how long it takes. That is if they are lucky enough to be placed temporarily in a no-kill shelter. Some are not so lucky. When you are a black animal these numbers change dramatically!

Why Choose a Black Dog

Ask any black dog owner and they will give you a list of reasons why choose to adopt a black dog. Here is a few Fur Friends has observed:

• Fiercely loyal
• Always appear cleaner than lighter color dogs
• Natural born heaters when snuggling
• Very mysterious (especially when hiding in a dark corner)
• People are more scared of darker colored dogs (given you a layer of protection)
• Though them may look similar—they have very distinct personalities

“Black Dog Syndrome” is real and has gone on long enough (read upcomong blog December 18)! Every black dog at our facility challenges you to get to know them and decide for yourself what makes them so special!

Why Choose a Black Cat

Black cats have an unusual bad luck stereotype that has been long associated with them. But that hasn’t always been. At one time they were actually believed to bring good luck. Here at Fur Friend we know how amazing black cats are! Here’s why:

• Black cats are known to resist diseases more
• Some black cats will actually change color after being exposed to sunlight
• Just as affectionate (if not more) than other felines
• Most have vivid yellow eyes
• Friendly and outgoing
• Quickly adaptable to new surroundings

Just like black dogs, there is also a “Black Cat Syndrome” that black cats get overlooked by potential adopters believing superstitions from long ago. It is time to end this myth once and for all (read upcomong blog December 27).

Please take the time and get to know the black cats that are available at your local shelter and see if won doesn’t when your heart!

Let’s Change the Fate of Black Animals

The current statistics are: shelters across the U.S. collectively euthanize 670,000 dogs, and 860,000 cats. That a staggering 1.5 million animals a year! If you were born with black fur your chances of being among that number rises dramatically.

I write this article, while my own personal mini panther is cuddled up next to me, nudging my arm for a scratch behind the ears periodically, while she purrs loudly. As any pet owner knows—every animal has their own distinct personality. Black animals are no exception!

After all, those who are known to prefer wearing black—are said to live colorful lives!

Why not apply that theory to the animals we adopt and open your heart to a black animal today!

Please check out our adoption pages (dogs and cats), along with our social media to see what black pet we have available, and consider adding a new family member to your home!
