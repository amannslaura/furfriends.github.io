Frequently Asked Questions at Fur Friends

What year did your organization start?

Our organization started in 2008 by the four board members were attending college together.

What should I do if I see a stray animal?

Try to capture it gently and not aggressively. See if it has any type of ID, such as a collar. If none is found take it to a local animal shelter to see it is microchipped. If circumstances allow care for it while posting a ost pet on social media platforms, community boards, and the local paper. Contact your local animal shelter.

My child has been asking for a pet—at what age should we get one?

Animals help teach children responsibility and empathy. Each child is different and matures at different ages. When a family adopts a pet it’s the responsibility of the parent(s) to make sure the animal is being properly looked after. It has been noted that pets can calm fears and anxiety, so if your child suffers from either of these—it may do a world of good to have something furry to cuddle.

Why do people abandon pets they no longer want?

Even though it is unclear why an individual would do this, we like to encourage people to reach out to organizations that could help place unwanted pets in forever homes.

I live in an apartment and work full-time should I get a pet?

Many apartments do welcome pets. Others unfortunately have a hard fast rule of no pets. Before deciding on adopting make sure your apartment building accepts pets. Some of the pets that find themselves at our shelter have been pet surrenders from individuals that could not keep their pets due to a “no pet rule”. Cats have been known to be a better choice because the convenience of a litter box for those who live in apartments.

How many animals do you currently have?

It fluctuates from day to day. Before our annual events we will receive some from neighboring shelters to try to place as many animals in their forever home as possible. To date we have never left to go home with an animal we brought and have a “wall of fame” at the shelter of all the happy animals and their new families.

I’d like to volunteer but my schedule changes often, could I still apply?

Yes! We can always use help and will try to accommodate needs as much as possible. Please be realistic when speaking to our shelter manager—she depends on helping hands.

What happens if a pet is not adopted after some time goes by?

Fur Friends is a no-kill animal shelter. Many of our animals have come from other facilities that they may not have had a chance of living to see their forever home become a reality. Every animal that comes to our shelter will stay until they find their forever families—until then they our one of our pets.

How do you spend donation money received?

We are a not-for profit organization. Donations go towards the care and health of the animals at our shelter and other organizations that we support such as UCANN that helps serve feral animals with catch-and-release programs to help slow cat population down.
