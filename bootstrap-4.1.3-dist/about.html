About Fur Friends


Fur Friends is a not-for-profit organization that depends on the generosity and support of others. Through individual, corporate sponsors, and community assistance we have helped place countless strays and unwanted animals in their forever homes. Currently each month we assist five dogs, ten puppies, nine cats, and twelve kittens homes in the greater Essex, Vermont area. Fur Friends is also involved in aiding other shelters in taking in hard to place and special needs animals—many of them arriving from out-of-state "kill" shelters.

Fur Friends has a 100 percent forever home adoption rate placements for these remarkable animals. Meaning our adopters our devoted to the care and love thay agree on when adopting these very special animals!

Periodically we will use Petfinder.com to alert our patrons of a unique opportunity to help with a special needs circumstance or dire situation. Our website is the best place to check on what animals we currently have—either here at the shelter or in one of our many foster homes that could directly transition straight to their forever home. Our organization will be working in the future to post our available animals through social media platforms updating them often. Please check back often to see what new animals we may have available for adoption—it is constantly changing!

Fur Friends relies on donations and volunteer help our community supplies. These donations not only help with the daily needs and medical care these animals require–they also help provide adoption fee subsidies, replace equipment, expand services(extra pens and fenced in areas), and building maintenance. We try to stretch every dollar in the best way possible to help these animals until they find a place to call home. Fur Friends is their temporary family and home on their quest in finding their forever home.

Fur Friends would not be possible without the devoted volunteers that help out continually time and again. They are the heart of the Fur Friends organization! We are truly indebted to them for all their hard work and support, including the many therapeutic foster placements—providing extra care to kittens and pets that have lost their elderly companions.


"Our mission is to find every animal a forever home where they will be cared for and treated like family."


Educating the community on good pet ownership responsibility and the importance of getting cats and dogs neutered and spayed to help control the pet population. This one act helps in aiding to finding every animal their forever home. Let's all do our part!
